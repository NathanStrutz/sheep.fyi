If you're teaching at SHEEP and need a website to put more information out there, I can host a small, fast website for it - your syllabus, notes, and links, all at your own subdomain. It's free, hosted on the internet's fastest web servers, and updating it is as easy as editing a text file online.

I have no business model. There's no money in it. We are families, and we are in it together.

It looks like this
05 steps
01
Send me your class
Email me your class, syllabus, and any other content - we can work on it together and you'll have freedom to keep going by yourself. This will be a rough outline. Browse the existing sites to see what's been done elsewhere. Don't worry too much about formatting.
02
Pick a subdomain
Your site lives at its own address, like your-class.sheep.fyi. Tell me what you'd like it to be.
03
Sign up for GitHub
The code for your website will be hosted on GitHub - a Microsoft company - sign up for free. Let me know your GitHub username. I will give you full access to edit your website.
04
I build & publish it
I turn your materials into a clean, plain-text (markdown) site and host it here. No surprises, no ads, no clutter - just your content, served quickly.
05
Update it anytime
Edit your own files whenever you want. I can show you how. Edits go live within a few minutes. You won't have to wait for me.

Scared? I can do it for you. Just send me the edits.
